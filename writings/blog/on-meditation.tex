When we talk about meditation, what do we actually mean?
Patanjali has defined 4 relevant steps towards Yoga, i.e. Pratyahara, Dharana, Dhyana, and Samadhi.
Now, according to Western philosophy Pratyahara could already define meditation as the inward movement of the attention, or as contemplation. Just like in the term "premeditate".
In \textit{Mindfulnes} meditation is probably Dharana - focusing the attention, while in Buddhism it would be Dhyana that best defines meditation, namely the cessation of thought.
Lastly, in Sahaja Yoga we talk of Samadhi when we say meditation, as that is the state of thoughtless awareness, which is enabled by the crossing of the Kundalini through the Agya.

Furthermore, when we talk about meditation we have to also explain what attention really is.
